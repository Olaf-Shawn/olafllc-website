Hawaii isn't a mainland market — and that's precisely the opportunity.

Distress in Hawaii doesn't arrive in a flood. The islands have long foreclosure timelines, strong borrower protections, structural housing undersupply, and a high cost basis with limited replacement inventory. The result is a slow-release market: distress emerges gradually, stays constrained, and rewards those with the patience and local relationships to source it deal by deal.

That dynamic is hard for outside capital to navigate and easy to misread from afar. It's also exactly the kind of market where deep island knowledge, hands-on sourcing, and disciplined underwriting create a real edge — turning a complex, slow-moving landscape into a steady stream of carefully selected opportunities.

Local Knowledge

Every island is a different market

One of the most misunderstood things about Hawaii real estate is that it isn't one market — each island has its own supply, pricing, and execution dynamics. We focus where the opportunity and the discipline line up.

O‘ahu · Honolulu

Largest volume, most competitive

The deepest pool of distressed property in the state — but also the most competitive, with firm pricing and institutional presence. Opportunity here demands selectivity and speed.

Hawai‘i Island

Growing volume, attractive entry

A moderate but growing distressed market with more attractive price points and real execution flexibility — often the sweet spot of volume and value.

Kaua‘i

Thin but selective

Limited inventory and higher price points mean thinner volume — but selective, well-chosen opportunities can still be found for those paying attention.

Discipline Over Reach

We don't chase every market

Knowing where not to operate matters as much as knowing where to. We concentrate where our knowledge and sourcing give us a genuine edge, and pass on the rest.

How We Operate

Sourced locally. Underwritten strictly.

Every asset moves through the same disciplined path — and nothing enters the portfolio without clearing the bar.

01

Source

Opportunities are identified and surfaced on the ground, through local relationships and active, hands-on sourcing across the islands we focus on.

02

Inspect & Evaluate

Every property — or the property securing a note — is personally inspected and evaluated. No deal moves forward on paper alone.

03

Underwrite & Approve

Each opportunity is underwritten against strict, pre-set criteria and brought to the managing members for approval. Discipline at the gate is the whole point.

04

Reposition & Resolve

Approved assets are improved, restructured, or resolved toward the right exit — managed actively from acquisition through disposition.
